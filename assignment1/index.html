First Impression

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing that caught my attention was the 3D graphics, which highlighted different sections of the interface. When I hovered over these graphics, they responded to my mouse movements, where several elements moved, and popup text followed my cursor. This made it clear that certain parts of the interface were interactive. I then noticed other touchable elements that reacted to my mouse, with a circle showing my movements getting larger, indicating that more elements were interactable. After this, I saw a prompt that said, "Scroll to explore the Luniverse" at the bottom of the homepage. When I scrolled, the 3D elements began floating, leading me to the next page. These interactive 3D elements were key in drawing me into the experience. Additionally, the minimalist design, clean layout, and calming color scheme of the homepage also stood out, making the interface both engaging and visually appealing.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Noticed the 3D graphics on the homepage : Hovered my mouse over and observing how they responded with the movements and popup.
  • Identified interactive elements : Clicked on the pen and book on the couch, which responded to my cursor, triggering an animation.
  • Scrolled through the homepage : Followed a prompt that encouraged me to, which caused the 3D elements to move and float across the screen and land on the "Omada" section
  • Played with interactive 3D elements : Clicked on several items in each section page and scrolled through until I ended up back in the homepage.

User Engagement

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

The part of the Luni.app experience where I spent the most time was with the 3D interactive elements on the homepage. These elements were not only visually attractive but also responded to my actions, keeping me engaged. Their movement and feedback when I hovered over them made me want to explore more and see how each one reacted to different actions. This part of the experience felt especially immersive, creating a sense of discovery as I uncovered new features with each interaction. The time I spent with these elements was the most engaging part of the experience, setting the tone for how I interacted with the rest of the website.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action during my two-minute interaction with the Luni.app website was hovering over different interactive elements to observe their responses. This included not only the 3D graphics on the homepage but also the navigation bar at the top of the page. Each time I hovered over an element, whether it was a graphic or a navigation section, there was visual feedback, such as movement, popup text, or preview images. This consistent feedback reinforced the idea that the site was designed to be explored through active interaction, keeping me engaged throughout the experience.

Experience Design

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

My impression of the main goal of the Luni.app experience is to immerse users in a lively and visually appealing environment that encourages exploration. The 3D graphics, interactive elements, and responsive design show that the site wants to capture users' attention and guide them through its content in an engaging and intuitive way. The interactivity isn't just for show - it's a key part of the experience. This makes it clear that the site is designed to keep users interested and make exploring the content enjoyable and memorable.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The Luni.app experience shows its main goal through responsive design and visual cues that guide how you interact. When you first see the homepage, the 3D graphics and hover effects grab your attention, showing that this isn't just a site to look at. The website uses prompts like "Scroll to explore the Luniverse" to encourage you to take action. As you interact, elements move and respond, keeping you engaged. This approach clearly shows that the website is meant to be explored and interacted with, not just viewed, making for a more immersive and engaging experience.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

I feel that the Luni.app experience is meant for short, repeated interactions over time. The interactive elements, dynamic visuals, and content layout suggest that users are encouraged to explore the site gradually, coming back to find new features or content with each visit. The navigation bar, with its preview images, indicates that there's always more to discover, keeping the site fresh and engaging even after several visits. Overall, the experience seems designed for users to engage in short bursts, exploring different parts of the site each time.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The Luni.app experience shows how to interact with it through its design and layout, which encourage exploration. The interactive elements and prompts guide you to take certain actions. The navigation bar with preview images hints that there's always more to discover. The way the site gradually reveals content and responds to your actions suggests that the experience is meant to unfold over several visits. This approach makes you want to come back and explore more each time, creating a feeling of ongoing discovery and engagement.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part of the interaction is when too many things respond at once. If lots of 3D graphics, popups, and other elements react to my actions at the same time, it's hard to focus on one thing or know what's important. This can be confusing and make the experience less enjoyable. It becomes difficult to use the site effectively when there's too much happening. While interactivity is good, it needs to be balanced so it doesn't overwhelm the user.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part of the interaction is how smooth and responsive the 3D elements are. Their movements and immediate feedback based on my actions make the experience feel intuitive and rewarding. Each interaction feels meaningful, and the visual responses show that the site reacts to my presence. This level of interactivity enhances the user experience, making it both engaging and enjoyable. I feel satisfied knowing that my actions have a direct and visible effect on the interface, creating a sense of control and immersion.
